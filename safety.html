Earthquake Safety

High Priority Skills

During Earthquake - DROP, COVER, HOLD ON

1

DROP

Drop to hands and knees immediately

2

COVER

Take cover under sturdy desk or table

3

HOLD ON

Hold on and protect your head and neck

Before Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls
  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy tables)
  • Keep emergency kit accessible
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, Hold On" with family
  • Know how to turn off gas, water, and electricity

After Earthquake

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Check for gas leaks, electrical damage
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Use phone only for emergencies

Flood Safety

Water Safety Critical

Remember: "Turn Around, Don't Drown!"

  • 6 inches of moving water can knock you down
  • 12 inches can carry away a vehicle
  • NEVER drive through flooded roads
  • Move to higher ground immediately when advised
  • Follow designated evacuation routes

Fire Safety

Time Critical - Seconds Count

Fire Evacuation: Stay Low, Get Out Fast!

  • Smoke rises - breathable air is near the floor
  • Test doors with back of hand before opening
  • Use stairs, NEVER elevators
  • Close doors behind you to slow fire spread
  • Go to designated meeting point

Storm Safety

Lightning & Wind Hazards

Lightning Safety: 30-30 Rule

  • Seek shelter if thunder follows lightning by 30 seconds or less
  • Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before going outside
  • Stay away from windows and glass doors
  • Avoid using electrical appliances
  • Don't use landline phones except for emergencies
